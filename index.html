📐 Grammar
be / get used to
Three forms — three meanings. Always followed by gerund (-ing).

The three meanings — from the diary

be used to + -ing

Feel something is normal right now

"I am used to eating dinner earlier."

get used to + -ing

Begin to feel comfortable (process over time)

"It took 3 weeks to get used to the hours."

not be used to + -ing

Still feel something is strange or uncomfortable

"I am not used to being away from home."

Key rule: After be/get used to — always use the gerund (-ing). Never use the infinitive.

Role play: 3:00
Student A — Exchange Student

You just arrived in Cali, Colombia. Ask your host family about food, schedules, greetings and customs. Say what surprises you. Use: "I am not used to..." / "I am getting used to..." / feeling words from today.

Student B — Colombian Host

Welcome the student! Explain local customs and habits. Reassure them. Use: "You will get used to..." / "Most people find it easy to get used to..." / "At first it might feel [word], but..."
